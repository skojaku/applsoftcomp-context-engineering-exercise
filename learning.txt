## Language Models are Few-Shot Learners + Brown et al. + 2020
This paper establishes that scale alone enables in-context learning—GPT-3's 175B parameters allow it to perform tasks via few-shot demonstrations without fine-tuning, sometimes matching SOTA fine-tuned models. The key mechanism is meta-learning: the model develops broad skills during pre-training, then uses them at inference time to adapt to tasks specified through text conditioning. However, performance varies widely—strong on translation, QA, and synthesis, but weak on NLI and comparison tasks, suggesting autoregressive models struggle with bidirectional reasoning.

## Context versus Prior Knowledge in Language Models + Du et al. + 2024
This paper introduces information-theoretic metrics (persuasion and susceptibility scores) that reveal entity familiarity determines context reliance—familiar entities resist contextual persuasion while unfamiliar ones are highly susceptible. This refines Brown 2020's observation about autoregressive models struggling with bidirectional reasoning: the issue is not just architectural but depends on training data frequency creating entity-specific priors that compete with in-context information. The finding that medium-sized models are most persuaded by assertive contexts (not largest models) suggests scale strengthens prior knowledge anchoring rather than context integration ability.
